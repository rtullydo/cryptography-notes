Algorithm 3.6.2. Naive.
Let \(p\) be a prime between \(2^k\) and \(2^{k+1}\text{.}\) The DLP in \(\F_p\ad\) is stated in terms of \(p, g, h\text{,}\) which comprises \(3k\) bits of storage. Thus, the problem can be stated in \(\mathcal O(k)\) bits. To find \(x\) so that
\begin{equation*} g^x \equiv h \bmod p, \end{equation*}
check every value of \(x\) from \(1, \ldots, p-1\text{.}\) That is, an \(\mathcal O(k)\) input requires an \(\mathcal O(p) = \mathcal O(2^k)\) steps to find; that is, the brute force is exponential.
