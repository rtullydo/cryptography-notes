Notice that we use nothing more than addition, subtraction, multiplication and division of integers in the calculation of the sum of points \(P, Q\text{.}\) These operations easily translate to the finite field setting, as long as we're careful abouy interpretation. For example, consider the calculation of \(m = \frac{y_2 - y_1}{x_2 - x_1}\) in \(\F_p\text{.}\) Interpreting fractions as multiplication by inverses in \(\F_p\text{,}\) we get

\begin{align*} m \amp= \frac{y_2 - y_1}{x_2 - x_1} \pmod p\\ \amp= (y_2 - y_1)\frac{1}{x_2 - x_1} \pmod p\\ \amp= (y_2 - y_1)(x_2 - x_1)\inv \pmod p. \end{align*}
