Theorem 2.5.4.
Let \(p\) be a prime. Every nonzero element \(a \in \Z_p\) has a multiplicative inverse, in the sense that there exists \(b\) so that
\begin{equation*} ab \equiv 1 \bmod p. \end{equation*}
Since \(b\) is unique, we denote \(b\) by \(a\inv \bmod p\) or just \(a\inv\) if the set in question is clear.
