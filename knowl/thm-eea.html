Theorem 2.2.5. (extended Euclidean algorithm).
Let \(a, b\) be positive integers. Then the equation
\begin{equation*} au + bv = \gcd(a,b) \end{equation*}
always has a solution in integers \(u\) and \(v\text{.}\) Moreover, given a solution \((u_0, v_0)\text{,}\) every solution \((u,v)\) is of the form
\begin{equation*} u = u_0 + \frac{bk}{\gcd(a,b)} \text{ and } v = v_0 - \frac{ak}{\gcd(a,b)} \text{ for } k \in \Z. \end{equation*}
