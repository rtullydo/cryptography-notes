Theorem 2.6.5. (Primitive root theorem).
Let \(p\) be a prime number. Then there exists an element \(g \in \F_p\ad\) whose powers give every element of \(F_p\ad\text{.}\) That is,
\begin{equation*} \F_p\ad = \{1, g, g^2, \ldots, g_{p-2}\}. \end{equation*}
Such a \(g\) is called a generator or primitive root of \(F_p\ad\text{.}\)
